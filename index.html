About Me

Hello World! My name is Isabel D'Alessandro. I'm a recent graduate of Wellesley College where I studied Neuroscience and Computer Science. Currently, I am working as a research assistant/ computational neuroanatomy technician in the lab of Dr. Rachel Wilson at Harvard Medical School. I'm particularly intersted in computational and systems neuroscience, and much of the research I've participated in has focused on elucidating how the brain integrates sensory information to drive complex behaviors. I'm also very interested in computational models of intelligence and like to think a lot about both how computers can allow us to gain insight into the human brain and how the human brain can inspire the creation of artificial intelligences. Feel free to browse this website to check out some of of the projects I've worked on, and research I've participated in.
